GMP & Regulatory Compliance

“Code Regulations, GMP Compliance, and the Industry”

The cGMP is the best regulatory practice that assists industry current and approved good manufacturing practice worldwide. It also provides excellent GMP Inspection training to auditors and inspectors for industrial alignment. Further, it motivates professionals in regulated industries for self-assessment and for continuous improvement. The Code and Regulatory requirements and the cGMP compliance initiatives are on our side.

Our Knowledge and Standard Professional Practices

  • Understanding and Applying Codes and Regulations to our standard project execution practice
  • Applying Quality by Design (QbD) to project initiating with predictable quality deliverables
  • Following Critical Quality Attributes (CQA) and securing the Critical Performance Parameters (CPP) during project development and execution
  • Utilization of Quality System Inspection Technology (QSIT) as self-check list for Regulatory Inspection readiness

Documentation and System Design Lifecycle (SDLC)

  • Developing Validation Master Plan (VMP) for project roadmap, resource planning, execution strategy and qualified deliverables
  • Applying commissioning, qualification and/or validation concept to qualify project and the acceptance of the deliverables
  • Identifying appropriate quality documents (SDLC) to secure the project deliverables
  • Turn-over completed project lifecycle documents to normal operations and to ensure business continuity

Projects and Records

  • Delivering GMP in Oral Solid Dose Form (OSD) Operational and Quality Control trainings to worldwide professionals from regulated industries, regulatory agencies, equipment vendors and service providers
  • Successfully completing biopharma facility compliance assessment and corrective action and preventive action plan (CAPA) in responding to FDA 483 “Inspectional Observations
  • Utilizing Quality by Design (QbD) and Design of Experiments (DOE) to assist new drug entity and drug substance (DS) components development
  • Assisting investigational events of Deviation Management (DM) to identify the process issues, operational deficiency, and technological advancement
